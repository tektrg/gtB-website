## Why Are People Switching from Grammarly? The Core Issues

Grammarly's popularity is undeniable, but it's not without its drawbacks. Here are the main reasons users are seeking alternatives:

- **Intrusive Subscription Model:** The free version is heavily restricted, and the premium plans can be costly, especially for students and individual users.
- **Privacy Concerns:** When you use Grammarly, your text is sent to their servers for analysis. For sensitive documents, this can be a major security risk. Many users are uncomfortable with a third party processing their private, professional, or creative writing.
- **Performance Lag:** Grammarly's browser extension can be resource-intensive, slowing down your browser and other applications, especially on platforms like Google Docs.
- **Limited Control:** You're locked into their ecosystem and their AI models. You have no control over the AI technology you use, which limits customization and flexibility. This is where **BYOM (Bring Your Own Model)** tools like GPT Breeze offer a massive advantage.
- **Overly Prescriptive:** Sometimes, Grammarly's suggestions can feel generic or even stifle your unique writing style.

These pain points are driving a shift towards more modern, flexible, and **privacy-focused writing tools**.

## Privacy & Cost: The Two Biggest Reasons to Switch

Let's break down the two most critical factors: your data and your money.

### The Privacy Problem
Grammarly's business model relies on processing your data on their servers. While they have security measures in place, the fundamental architecture means your information leaves your machine.

**Grammarly's Data Flow:**
`Your Computer → Grammarly's Servers (for processing) → Your Computer`

This creates a potential vulnerability. For legal documents, confidential business plans, or private journals, this is a risk many are unwilling to take.

**The GPT Breeze Privacy Model (True BYOM):**
`Your Computer → Your Chosen AI Provider (e.g., OpenAI)`

With GPT Breeze, your text is sent **directly** to the AI provider you choose. We act as a secure interface, but we never see, store, or process your data. This is the gold standard for privacy in the AI age.
